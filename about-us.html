COMMITMENTS & SPONSORSHIP

UNWTO Global Code of Ethics for Tourism

On April 4th, 2017 Caucasus Travel signed the document of Private Sector Commitment to the UNWTO Global Code of Ethics for Tourism. Caucasus Travel is among five hundred sixty private enterprises Worldwide, that joined the Global Code of Ethics and Corporate responsibility. We were honoured to receive that document from hands of Mr. Taleb Rifai, Secretary General of UNWTO.

Actually, we committed to that criteria, by heart, much earlier, from very first steps of our Company

At the times, when Caucasus Travel was established, Georgia entered one of the most turbulent times of its history. As a result of ethnic conflicts and Russian occupation, collapse of economy, ethnic cleansing and hundreds of thousands of Internally Displaced Persons and many other unfortunate developments, Georgia’s tourism infrastructure was entirely destroyed. Caucasus Travel found itself not only a pioneer of post-Intourist tourism world, but also developer of human capital and capacity builder of rural and remote area communities. Company rushed in to discovering and bringing on board motivated, hard-working people - home and vehicle owners in Tbilisi and regions, now job-less scientists, academics, professors, teachers, young villagers, farmers, etc, creating its own supply circle, which, apparently became foundation for incoming tourism in Georgia. There was no Corporate Social Responsibility meaning at that times – certainly not in the country with just very few emerging private businesses, however, we are proud of our deeds.

Free Training for Tour Guides

For 30 years, every year, we regularly making selection of 20-30 appropriate profiled candidates and providing them professional training for Tour Guiding. We do it free and without burdening them with preconditions to work only for us. We usually employ best of them. Most of them are working with us since their first training and we appreciate their input in our success.

Georgian State Mountain Guide School

We are proud that Caucasus Travel supported project of Mountain Guide School have developed to national scale enterprise - Adventure Tourism School. School predecessors and founders (Georgian Guide Service – renamed as Caucasus Travel), Georgian Mountain Guide School and Georgian Mountain Guide Association) are developing mountain guiding institutions since 1991. Hundreds of young people from mountain areas were trained over past 30 years. Most successful one made to the UIAGM guide standards. Since 2016, ATS is State-authorized TVET provider in Adventure tourism and safety management. Programs include training of trekking, alpine, ski and mountain guides, ski and SPRAW instructors. The curriculum is developed according to UIAGM standards.

The First International Expedition of Disabled persons on Mt. Kazbek (5047m)

On October 1995 four disabled people climbed Mt. Kazbek. The initiators and organizers of this unique expedition were Caucasus Travel and the Georgian Mountain Guides. The excellent coordination and the high level of professionalism of the team allowed the success of this highly risky and unique expedition.
